1
Viriboni (Varnam)
Bhairavi · Ata Talam · Pacchimiriyam Adiyappa · Telugu
▼
Ragam: Bhairavi
Talam: Ata (14 beats)
Language: Telugu
Aro: S G₂ R₂ G₂ M₁ P D₂ N₂ Ṡ
Ava: Ṡ N₂ D₂ P M₁ G₂ R₂ S
A Varnam is a dense, comprehensive composition that serves as the opening declaration of a concert. It explores all the characteristic phrases and movements of the ragam, covering pallavi, anupallavi, muktyi swaram, chitta swaram, and charanam. Bhairavi is one of Carnatic music's most richly emotive ragams — soulful and yearning, it is also the parent of the beloved Hindustani Bhairavi.

Meaning
Notation
Pallavi

Anupallavi
Shirasu au dakshinA dvArakA sAmi shrI rAjagOpAla dEvA
Charanam
chirunavvu mOmuna
Central theme: This varnam expresses the Shringaara rasa (romantic devotion) of the heroine Viriboni for Lord Rajagopala of Tanjapuri. Through the metaphor of a flower blooming, the heroine describes her devotion — she has become enchanted (marulukonnadirA = I am bewildered/enchanted) by Him. The composition expresses the universal theme in Tamil devotion: the devotee as a lovelorn heroine seeking union with the divine.
Pallavi

Anupallavi
O Lord of the sacred south, O Lord of Dwaraka, O Lord Rajagopala Deva.
Charanam
With a gentle smile (chirunavvu) on your face (mOmuna)…

2
Shri Mahā Gaṇapati Rāvatu Mām
Gowlai · Misra Chapu · Mutthuswami Dikshitar · Sanskrit
▼
Ragam: Gowlai (Gowla)
Talam: Misra Chapu (7 beats)
Language: Sanskrit
Mudra: Guruguha (Dikshitar's signature)
Sung as an invocation to Mahā Gaṇapati before the Kamalāmbā Navavarṇa krithis, this composition references the Ganapati known as Mattu Uraitta Pillayar, whose temple stands on the bank of Kamalālayam — the temple tank in Tiruvarur. The legend of Lord Tyagaraja helping devotee Sundara Moorthy Nayanar obtain gold through this Ganapati is embedded in the notes.

Pallavi
May Lord Mahā Gaṇapati protect me (rāvatu māṁ). He is Siddhi Vināyaka, the elephant-faced (Mātaṅga Mukha) one.
Anupallavi
Venerated (sannuta) by Vishnu (Kāmajanaka — father of Kama), Brahma (Vidhi), and Indra (Indra); He resides (nivāso) on the banks (taṭa) of the temple-tank Kamalālaya; His feet (pāda) and hands (kara) are beautiful like tender (komala) leaves (pallava). He is the elder brother (āgrajah) of Guruguha and the son of Shiva.
Charanam
He is attractive like pure gold and is the remover of impediments. His feet are like the lotus. He is attired in white, his forehead is like a crescent moon. Lambodhara holds his own tusk, a lily, a rope, a goad, and a Modaka. He is the boat (nāvo) that takes us across the ocean of Saṁsāra. His body shines like a thousand suns. He has a mouse as his mount. Hosts of celestials worship him and he abides in eternal bliss (Kaivalya).
Legend: Sundara Moorthy Nayanar sought gold from Lord Tyagaraja to make jewels for his wife. Five golden bricks were left at a kiln near Keevalur; returning home to Tiruvarur, he found them in the Kamalālaya tank. When goldsmiths doubted their purity, Lord Ganesha appeared disguised as Muthanna Achari and made them shine as the purest gold. This Ganapati is known as Mattu Uraitta Pillayar.

Jagadānandha Kāraka
Naatai · Adi Talam · St. Thyagaraja · Sanskrit
▼
Ragam: Naatai (36th mela janyam)
Talam: Adi (8 beats)
Aro: S R3 G3 M1 P D3 N3 S
Ava: S N3 P M1 R3 S
Series: Pancharatna Krithi #1
Pancharatna Krithi: One of five gem compositions by Thyagaraja, performed en masse at annual Aradhana festivals. This first gem contains 108 names (ashtottara) of Lord Rama embedded across its charanams. Thyagaraja describes Shri Rama as Parabrahma — greater than the Trinity — and as bestower of happiness to all the world.

Sūrya Mūrtē
Sowrastram · Dhruva Talam · Mutthuswami Dikshitar · Sanskrit
▼
Ragam: Sowrastram (Sūryakāntam janya)
Talam: Dhruva (14 beats)
Aro: S R₁ G₃ M₁ P M₁ D₂ N₃ Ṡ
Ava: Ṡ N₃ D₂ N₂ D₂ P M₁ G₃ R₁ S
Series: Navagraha Krithi #1 (Surya)
The first of Dikshitar's nine planetary compositions (Navagraha Krithis). Since Surya is described as Namaskāra-priya (one who likes salutations), the krithi opens with namōstu tē — salutations to you. The composition is in the eighth vibhakti (vocative case), addressing Surya directly throughout. Dikshitar's mudra "Guruguha" is embedded in the charanam.

5
Kā Vā Vā
Varali · Adi Talam · Papanasam Shivan · Tamil
▼
Ragam: Varali
Talam: Adi (8 beats)
Language: Tamil
Deity: Lord Muruga / Shanmugha
Papanasam Shivan (1890–1973), revered as the "Tamil Thyagaraja," composed this urgent invocation to Lord Muruga. The title itself — Kā Vā Vā — means "Come, protect (me), come." Varali is a ragam of intense longing and deeply haunting beauty, perfectly matched to the pleading urgency of this appeal.

Rāghuvamsha Sudhāmbudhi Chandra
Kathanakuthoohalam · Adi Talam · Patnam Subramanya Iyer · Telugu
▼
Ragam: Kathanakuthoohalam
Talam: Adi (8 beats)
Aro: Sa Re Ma Dha Ni Ga Pa Sa
Ava: Sa Ni Dha Pa Ma Ga Re Sa
Language: Telugu
Kathanakuthoohalam means "eagerness to narrate wonders" — a ragam known for its energising, martial quality. It is believed this krithi was historically sung to kings to motivate them before battle. The fast-paced number in this vigorous ragam makes it equally compelling on violin, veena, and saxophone.

7
O Raṅgashāyi — Main Piece
Khambhoji · Adi Talam (2 kalai) · St. Thyagaraja · Telugu
▼
Ragam: Khambhoji (28th mela janyam)
Talam: Adi 2-kalai (16 counts)
Aro: S R2 G3 M1 P D2 S
Ava: S N2 D2 P M1 G3 R2 S N3 P D2 S
Tonight's centrepiece. This krithi will be presented with full raga alapanai (free improvisation), neraval (lyrical improvisation within a line), and kalpana swaram (solfège exchanges). Thyagaraja addresses Lord Raṅganātha reclining at Shrī Raṅgam, asking why He remains silent when sincerely called. Khambhoji — grand, warm, and romantic — is among the most beloved ragams for a main piece.

Entamātramuna Evvaru Talachina
Ragamalika · Misra Chapu · Annamacharya · Telugu
▼
Ragam: Ragamalika (multiple ragams)
Talam: Misra Chapu (7 beats)
Language: Telugu
Deity: Lord Venkateswara (Tirupati)
15th-century poet-saint Annamacharya (1408–1503), the first known composer of Telugu keertanas, composed this profound philosophical statement. The central idea: God's form and grace are shaped entirely by the devotee's own imagination and devotion — all paths, all forms of worship, all names lead to the same Supreme Being.

Oh Venkatapati, you are (nīvu) exactly (anta mātramē) as much (entamātramuna) as whoever (evvaru) thinks of you (talachina). When I observe sincerely, it is just like the size and quality of a pancake depending on the batter.

Om Namo Nārāyaṇa (with Viruttham)
Karnaranjani · Khanda Chapu · Ambujam Krishna · Tamil
▼
Ragam: Karnaranjani
Talam: Khanda Chapu (5 beats)
Language: Tamil
Deity: Guruvayurappan (Lord Vishnu)
Preceded by a Viruttham — Thirumangai Azhwar's verse "Kulam Tharum Selvam Thandhidum" set freely in ragam without talam.
